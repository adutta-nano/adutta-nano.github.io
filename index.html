I am currently pursuing my MS in Nanoscience and Nanotechnology at KU Leuven, Belgium and TU Dresden, Germany as an Erasmus Mundus Scholar. My research interests span nanoelectronics, advanced logic and memory devices, 2D materials, electron transport, and condensed matter physics.

Previously, I spent a year working as a Research Engineer in the Department of Condensed Matter Physics at the Tata Institute of Fundamental Research (TIFR) on Josephson Junctions and 2D material heterostructures. I've also had the opportunity to work as a joint intern between Intel Components Research Oregon and IMEC Leuven on stress modeling in Complementary Field Effect Transistors (CFETs).

When I'm not squinting my eyes at a research paper, I like to spend my time outdoors hiking, traveling, and exploring eateries. I'm also a big sports fan (Hala Madrid!!), dog lover, and foodie!

Current Research

Master's Thesis • Ultrafast Microscopy Lab
TU Dresden, Germany • Prof. Alexey Chernikov
Nov 2024 - Present
Hyperspatial Second-Harmonic Microscopy of Strain Fields in 2D Semiconductors
  • Combining angle-resolved second-harmonic generation (SHG) and photoluminescence (PL) to map spatially modulated strain tensors in monolayer TMDs
  • Investigating the effect of strain on optical selection rules for excitons and studying temperature dependence of strain fields
  • Characterizing formation of strain vortices and quasi-1D excitonic waveguides
  • Performing numerical simulations of exciton diffusion in strain-induced energy landscapes
